> [!the-purpose]
> This topic set bridges the gap between your theoretical understanding of cognitive and educational psychology and the practical challenge of daily planning within your Personal Knowledge Base. These topics examine planning not as a productivity hack, but as a cognitively-informed system that aligns with how your mind actually works. Each topic explores the deep relationship between specific cognitive principles you've been studying (self-regulation, metacognition, cognitive load) and the architectural decisions required to make planning sustainable, effective, and learning-oriented. The focus is on understanding WHY certain planning approaches succeed or fail based on cognitive architecture, HOW to design PKB-based planning systems that work WITH your cognitive constraints rather than against them, and WHAT makes planning an instrument of continuous self-knowledge rather than just task management. Together, these topics provide a comprehensive framework for transforming your Obsidian vault into an active planning system grounded in cognitive science.
