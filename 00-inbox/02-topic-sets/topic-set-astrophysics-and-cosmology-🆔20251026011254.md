### Further Exploration

To deepen your strategic understanding of the interconnected nature of these fields, consider these adjacent and highly relevant subtopics for future investigation:

  - **Weak Gravitational Lensing:** A methodology for mapping the distribution of Dark Matter, which warps the light from background galaxies.
  - **The Sunyaev-Zel'dovich Effect:** A phenomenon that allows for the detection of massive galaxy clusters through the scattering of CMB photons.
  - **Redshift-Space Distortions (RSDs):** A technique used in large-scale galaxy surveys to disentangle the effects of cosmological expansion from the peculiar motions of galaxies caused by local gravity.
  - **Planetary Migration Theory:** The mechanisms (e.g., disc-planet interaction) that explain why many exoplanets, like "Hot Jupiters," are found in orbits that challenge classical in-situ formation models.
