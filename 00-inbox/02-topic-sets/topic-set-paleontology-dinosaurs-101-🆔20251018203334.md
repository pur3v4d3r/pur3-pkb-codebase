## Further Exploration

To deepen your understanding of these topics, you may find the following related subtopics valuable for further investigation.

- **Ontogenetic Niche Partitioning:** The study of how a dinosaur's ecological role (e.g., its prey) changed as it grewâ€”for example, how a juvenile *T. rex* hunted different animals than its parents, effectively competing with smaller predators.
- **Dinosaur Paleopathology:** The study of injuries and diseases in dinosaurs. Healed fractures, bite marks, and infections preserved in fossils provide powerful clues about their behavior and interactions.
- **The "Warm-Blooded" Debate:** An exploration of the evidence for **endothermy** (warm-bloodedness) in dinosaurs, including their rapid growth rates, bone histology, and predator-prey ratios.
- **Melanosome Preservation:** A cutting-edge field that analyzes preserved pigment-bearing organelles (melanosomes) in fossil feathers to reconstruct the *actual colors* of dinosaurs.
