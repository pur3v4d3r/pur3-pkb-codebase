# Further Exploration

To deepen your understanding of the Large-Scale Structure, you may find the following related topics and subtopics valuable for further investigation.

- **Redshift-Space Distortions:** An exploration of how the peculiar velocities of galaxies (their motion relative to the Hubble flow) distort our maps of the LSS and how cosmologists correct for and use this distortion to measure the growth of structure.
- **The Sunyaev-Zel'dovich Effect:** A study of how the hot gas in galaxy clusters within the cosmic web interacts with the Cosmic Microwave Background radiation, providing another method to detect and study these massive structures.
- **Weak Gravitational Lensing:** An investigation into how the mass of the cosmic web, both dark and luminous, subtly bends the light from background galaxies, allowing cosmologists to map the distribution of matter without relying on visible tracers like galaxies.
- **Cosmological Simulations:** A look into the computational techniques behind large-scale N-body simulations (like Illustris, EAGLE, or Millennium) that model the formation of the cosmic web from first principles, providing a theoretical framework to compare with observations.
