## Further Exploration

To deepen your understanding of the field, you may find the following related subtopics valuable for further investigation.

- **Ichnology:** The study of **trace fossils** (like footprints, burrows, and tooth marks), which provides a direct record of animal *behavior* rather than just their bodies.
- **Paleoclimatology:** The science of reconstructing past climates using "proxies" like ice cores, tree rings, and the chemical composition of marine fossils.
- **Dinosaur-Bird Transition:** An exploration of the overwhelming fossil evidence (e.g., feathered dinosaurs) that reveals modern birds are the direct descendants of theropod dinosaurs.
- **Stratigraphy:** The study of rock layers (strata). This is the foundational tool paleontologists use to place fossils in their correct geological order and establish a "relative" timeline of life on Earth.
