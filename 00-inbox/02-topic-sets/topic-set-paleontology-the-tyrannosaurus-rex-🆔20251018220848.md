### Further Exploration

To deepen your understanding of *Tyrannosaurus rex*, you may find these highly active areas of research valuable for further investigation:

* **The *Nanotyrannus* Debate:** A fierce taxonomic dispute over whether certain small, slender fossils belong to a distinct species (*Nanotyrannus lancensis*) or are simply juvenile *T. rex* specimens. This debate has massive implications for our understanding of *T. rex* ontogeny and tyrannosaur diversity.
* **Integument (Feathers vs. Scales):** While *T. rex's* ancestors were clearly feathered, fossilized skin impressions from *T. rex* itself show scales. This has led to the hypothesis that it was **secondarily scaly**, perhaps losing its feathers as it evolved a massive body size to better regulate heat, much like modern elephants.
* **Paleopathology:** The study of injuries and diseases in fossils. Healed *T. rex* bite marks on prey and evidence of injuries (like broken and healed bones) on *T. rex* skeletons themselves provide direct, tangible evidence of their behavior and the brutal nature of their lives.
