### Further Exploration

To deepen your understanding of this domain, you may find the following related topics and subtopics valuable for building your foundational knowledge:

* **The Zettelkasten Method:** An exploration of the principles of atomicity, autonomy, and linking developed by Niklas Luhmann, which forms the theoretical basis for modern atomic note-taking.
* **Evergreen Notes:** A study of the principles behind writing durable, concept-oriented notes that are designed to evolve and compound in value over time.
* **Cognitive Load Theory:** An investigation into the psychological principles that explain why managing information in "chunks" (as MOCs allow) is essential for effective learning and thinking.
* **Progressive Summarization:** A look at the specific technique for processing literature notes, which often serves as the raw material for synthesizing ideas within an MOC.
* **The "Linking Your Thinking" (LYT) Framework:** An analysis of the specific, popular methodology that heavily operationalizes the concept of MOCs as the primary interface for a PKB.
