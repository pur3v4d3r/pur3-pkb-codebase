Title: Foundational Principles: First-Order Topics in Cosmology and Astrophysics

> [!the-purpose]
> This topic set presents seven essential **first-principle concepts** across general relativity, stellar evolution, and cosmology. These topics are designed to serve as foundational knowledge for the more advanced research questions previously provided. The focus is on the core 'Why' and 'How' of key phenomena: the geometry of spacetime, the energy production of stars, the evidence for an expanding universe, and the basic physics of light that serves as our primary cosmic messenger. Mastering these concepts will provide the necessary theoretical and observational grounding to fully appreciate the complex interactions of Dark Matter, Black Hole mergers, and Galactic Evolution.

**Scope & Angle:** This topic focuses on the **Doppler Effect** as applied to light from distant galaxies, a foundational principle for modern cosmology. It examines the historical context of its discovery (Hubble's Law), the **mechanism** of its interpretation as cosmological expansion (not just recession velocity), and its **evidence** as the primary tool for determining the scale, age, and accelerating fate of the universe.

```markdown
A Foundational Analysis of Cosmological Redshift, Detailing the Mechanism by which the Expansion of Spacetime Alters Photon Wavelengths, and its Role as the Primary Observational Evidence for Hubble's Law and an Accelerating Universe
```
