# Further Exploration

As an Academic Curator, I would be remiss if I did not point out adjacent avenues of inquiry that would enrich your 6,000-word report. Any of the topics above could be strengthened by incorporating these concepts:

  * **Schema Theory:** This is the other half of the CLT equation. Understanding *how* schemas are formed, organized, and automated is critical.
  * **Dual Coding Theory (Paivio):** This pairs perfectly with the "modality effect" and "split-attention effect," offering a framework for how to effectively combine text and visuals in your notes.
  * **Spaced Repetition & Interleaving:** These are retrieval-practice techniques that directly relate to managing cognitive load over *time* to build robust, long-term schemas.
  * **Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky):** While from a different tradition (sociocultural), this concept of a "sweet spot" for learning maps directly onto the idea of managing *intrinsic load*â€”keeping tasks difficult enough to be germane, but not so difficult as to be overwhelming.
