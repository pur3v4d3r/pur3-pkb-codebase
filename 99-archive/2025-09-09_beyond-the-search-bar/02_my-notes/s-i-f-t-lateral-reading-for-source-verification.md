# SIFT Method for Information Evaluation

The SIFT method, developed by Mike Caulfield, is a framework for quickly evaluating online information by shifting from deep single-source analysis to lateral fact-checking.

## Core Principles

*   **Purpose:** To quickly and effectively assess the reliability and context of online information.
*   **Approach:** Encourages lateral reading and external verification rather than intense scrutiny of a single source.

## The SIFT Steps

### S - Stop
*   **Pause and Reflect:** Before engaging or sharing, take a moment to consider the content.
*   **Assess Source Reputation:** Ask if you know the source; if not, investigate before proceeding.
*   **Check Emotional Response:** Be highly skeptical of content designed to provoke strong emotions, as it's often manipulative.

### I - Investigate the Source
*   **Lateral Reading:** Open a new tab to search for information about the author, publication, or organization.
*   **Goal:** Quickly determine their **expertise** and **agenda**.
*   **"Wikipedia Trick":** Use Wikipedia for a fast overview of a source's history, ownership, funding, and biases.
*   **Example:** Understand the context if a claim about milk benefits comes from the dairy industry.

### F - Find Better (or Trusted) Coverage
*   **Prioritize Claims:** If a claim is important, focus on finding reliable information about it, not on debunking a low-quality source.
*   **Ignore Low-Quality Sources:** Rather than analyzing questionable sources, seek out trusted reporting on the same topic.
*   **Strategy:** Search for coverage from **multiple, high-quality sources** (e.g., major news agencies, scientific organizations).

### T - Trace Claims to the Original Source
*   **Combat Secondhand Information:** Much online information is quoted, summarized, or clipped, which can distort context.
*   **Go "Upstream":** Find the primary source of the information.
*   **Applications:**
    *   For research findings, locate the **original study**.
    *   For video clips, find the **full video** for original context.
*   **Red Flag:** Be wary of sources that make claims without providing links or references to the original information.
