## Examples of Verified IFCN Signatories

The following organizations are recognized IFCN signatories, each contributing to the field of fact-checking with their distinct areas of focus:

*   **FactCheck.org:** A nonpartisan project of the Annenberg Public Policy Center, focusing on reducing deception and confusion in U.S. politics.
*   **PolitiFact:** Known for its "Truth-O-Meter," it rates the accuracy of claims made by politicians and advocacy groups.
*   **Snopes:** One of the oldest fact-checking sites, specializing in debunking urban legends, internet rumors, and viral claims.
*   **Associated Press (AP) Fact Check:** The fact-checking arm of the global news agency, providing nonpartisan checks on news claims worldwide.
*   **Reuters Fact Check:** The fact-checking unit of the international news organization Reuters, verifying viral social media content and news claims.
*   **Agence France-Presse (AFP) Fact Check:** An international fact-checking operation by the global news agency AFP, working in multiple languages.
*   **Lead Stories:** Specializes in tracking and debunking trending fake news early, often before it goes viral, using proprietary technology.
*   **Check Your Fact:** An editorially independent project focusing on claims made by public figures and viral hoaxes.



# IFCN Signatories

The **International Fact-Checking Network (IFCN)** is a unit of the Poynter Institute dedicated to promoting excellence in fact-checking worldwide. Its primary function is to establish and uphold a **Code of Principles**, which serves as a benchmark for high-quality, nonpartisan fact-checking.

## What is an IFCN Signatory?

An IFCN signatory is an organization that has undergone a rigorous independent assessment and demonstrated adherence to the IFCN's Code of Principles. This signifies a commitment to:

*   **Nonpartisanship and Fairness:** Fact-checks are conducted without bias towards any political party, ideology, or individual.
*   **Transparency of Sources:** Fact-checkers clearly cite all sources used in their work, allowing readers to verify information.
*   **Transparency of Funding & Organization:** Information about the organization's funding sources and leadership is publicly available.
*   **Transparency of Methodology:** The process used to select, research, and publish fact-checks is clearly explained.
*   **Open and Honest Corrections Policy:** A clear policy for promptly correcting significant errors is in place and easily accessible.

Organizations that meet these standards are considered the **"gold standard"** in the field, assuring readers of their credibility and commitment to accuracy.
