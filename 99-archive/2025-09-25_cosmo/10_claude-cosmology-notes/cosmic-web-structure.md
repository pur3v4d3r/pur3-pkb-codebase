# Cosmic Web Structure

> *The universe's grandest architecture - a vast network of dark matter filaments connecting galaxies across cosmic space*

## üï∏Ô∏è The Universe's Skeleton

The Cosmic Web is the largest-scale structure in the universe, consisting of vast filaments of [[Dark Matter]] and gas that connect galaxies across hundreds of millions of light-years. Like a three-dimensional spider web, it forms the backbone along which matter flows and [[Galaxy Formation|galaxies form]], creating the universe's most magnificent architecture.

## üèóÔ∏è Components of the Cosmic Web

### 1. **Filaments** - The Highways of the Universe
- **Structure**: Long, thin threads of dark matter and gas
- **Length**: 50-200 Megaparsecs (163-650 million light-years)
- **Width**: 5-10 Megaparsecs across
- **Density**: 2-5 times the cosmic average
- **Role**: Matter flows along filaments toward galaxy clusters

### 2. **Nodes** - Cosmic Cities
- **Location**: Where multiple filaments intersect
- **Content**: Galaxy clusters and superclusters
- **Density**: 10-1000 times cosmic average
- **Examples**: Coma Cluster, Perseus Cluster, Local Supercluster
- **Formation**: Traffic jams where matter accumulates

### 3. **Walls** - Cosmic Sheets
- **Structure**: Thin, extended sheets of matter
- **Thickness**: ~5 Megaparsecs
- **Extent**: Can span 100+ Megaparsecs
- **Density**: 1.5-3 times cosmic average
- **Galaxies**: Often arranged in sheet-like distributions

### 4. **Voids** - The Empty Spaces
- **Size**: 20-150 Megaparsecs diameter
- **Density**: 10-20% of cosmic average
- **Volume**: ~80% of total space
- **Content**: Very few galaxies, mostly empty
- **Evolution**: Continuously expanding and emptying

## üî¨ Dark Matter vs. Baryonic Structure

### Dark Matter Web ([[Dark Matter]])
- **Dominance**: ~85% of all matter
- **Early Formation**: Clustering begins immediately after recombination
- **Collisionless**: Only interacts gravitationally
- **Smooth Distribution**: Forms continuous filamentary network
- **Detection**: Via gravitational lensing, galaxy motions

### Baryonic Web (Ordinary Matter)
- **Minority**: ~15% of total matter
- **Complex Physics**: Heating, cooling, pressure, chemistry
- **Observable**: Through galaxies, gas, X-ray emission
- **Thermal Broadening**: Pressure smooths small-scale structure
- **Feedback**: Stars and black holes affect distribution

### The Warm-Hot Intergalactic Medium (WHIM)
- **Location**: Shock-heated gas in filaments
- **Temperature**: 10‚Åµ - 10‚Å∑ K (warm-hot phase)
- **Fraction**: ~40% of ordinary matter at z = 0
- **Detection**: X-ray absorption, emission lines
- **Missing Baryons**: Long-sought reservoir of ordinary matter

- **Cluster Connections**: Hot gas bridges between clusters

## üåå Environmental Effects on [[Galaxy Formation]]

### Filament Galaxies
- **Star Formation**: Enhanced along filament spines
- **Morphology**: Often spiral galaxies
- **Orientation**: Aligned with filament direction
- **Accretion**: Steady gas supply from cosmic web

### Cluster Galaxies  
- **Transformation**: Spirals ‚Üí ellipticals via interactions
- **Star Formation**: Quenched by hot cluster gas
- **Ram Pressure**: Strips gas from infalling galaxies
- **Morphology-Density**: Relation reflects environment

### Void Galaxies
- **Rare**: Very low number density
- **Properties**: Often blue, star-forming, low mass
- **Evolution**: Slow, undisturbed evolution
- **Unique**: Laboratories for galaxy formation physics

### Large-Scale Flows
- **Peculiar Velocities**: Galaxies move toward overdensities
- **Bulk Flow**: Coherent motions on large scales
- **Assembly**: Matter streams along filaments
- **Observable**: Redshift-space distortions
