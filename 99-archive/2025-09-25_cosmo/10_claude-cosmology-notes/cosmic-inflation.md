## üéØ The Problems Inflation Solves

### 1. The Horizon Problem ([[The Horizon Problem]])
- **Issue**: Why is [[Cosmic Microwave Background|CMB]] temperature uniform across regions that were never in causal contact?
- **Inflation Solution**: 
  - Distant regions **were** in causal contact before inflation
  - Inflation stretched causally connected region beyond observable universe
  - Explains 2.725 K uniformity to 1 part in 100,000

### 2. The Flatness Problem ([[The Flatness Problem]])  
- **Issue**: Why is universe geometry so precisely flat (Œ© = 1.000...)?
- **Inflation Solution**:
  - Any initial curvature gets exponentially diluted
  - Like inflating a balloon - surface appears flat locally
  - Makes flatness generic outcome, not fine-tuned coincidence

### 3. The Monopole Problem
- **Issue**: Grand Unified Theories predict stable magnetic monopoles
- **Observation**: No monopoles detected despite intensive searches
- **Inflation Solution**: Dilutes monopole density to undetectable levels

### 4. The Smoothness Problem  
- **Issue**: Why is early universe so homogeneous and isotropic?
- **Inflation Solution**: Inflates small, smooth patch to cosmic size
- **Result**: Observable universe inherits smoothness of tiny region

## üåå Types of Inflation Models

### Slow-Roll Inflation (Most Common)
- **Mechanism**: Scalar field slowly rolls down potential
- **Duration**: ~60 e-folds of expansion
- **End**: Field reaches bottom of potential, oscillates
- **Reheating**: Field energy converts to standard particles

### Chaotic Inflation
- **Initial Conditions**: Large field values, random initial conditions
- **Advantage**: Doesn't require special initial state
- **Evolution**: Field rolls from large values toward minimum
- **Generic**: Happens in most regions of field configuration space

### Hybrid Inflation
- **Two Fields**: Inflaton and waterfall field
- **Ending**: Secondary field triggers end of inflation
- **Flexibility**: Can produce various observational signatures
- **Complexity**: More parameters but more realistic
