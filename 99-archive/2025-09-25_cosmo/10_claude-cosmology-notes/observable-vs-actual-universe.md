# Observable vs Actual Universe

> *What we can see is just a tiny fraction of what actually exists - our cosmic island in an infinite ocean*

The observable universe is the spherical region around Earth from which light has had time to reach us since the [[Timeline of the Universe|Big Bang]]. But this represents only a minute fraction of the actual universe, which may be infinite in size and contains countless regions forever beyond our view.
