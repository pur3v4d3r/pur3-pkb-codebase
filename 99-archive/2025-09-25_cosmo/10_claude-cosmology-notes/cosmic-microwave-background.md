# Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)

> *The afterglow of creation - light from when the universe first became transparent*

## üåå The Universe's Baby Picture

The Cosmic Microwave Background is thermal radiation left over from the [[Timeline of the Universe|recombination era]] when the universe was only 380,000 years old. It's the oldest light we can observe and provides our most direct view of the early universe.

## üîç The Discovery Story

### Accidental Discovery (1965)
- **Arno Penzias & Robert Wilson**: Working with radio antenna at Bell Labs
- **Problem**: Persistent background "noise" in their receiver  
- **Temperature**: Equivalent to 3.5 K blackbody radiation
- **Realization**: This was the predicted cosmic background radiation!
- **Nobel Prize**: Physics 1978 for this discovery

### Theoretical Prediction
- **Ralph Alpher & Robert Herman** (1948): Predicted ~5 K background radiation
- **George Gamow**: Developed early Big Bang nucleosynthesis theory
- **Connection**: Hot early universe should leave thermal remnant

### Physical Origins

#### Sachs-Wolfe Effect
- **Gravitational Redshift**: Photons climbing out of potential wells
- **Large Scales**: Dominated by gravitational effects
- **Signature**: Hot and cold spots from density variations

#### Acoustic Oscillations  
- **Sound Waves**: Pressure waves in primordial plasma
- **Driving**: Gravity vs radiation pressure
- **Freezing**: Oscillations frozen when universe became transparent
- **Scale**: Characteristic size ~1¬∞ on sky

#### Doppler Effect
- **Motion**: Bulk motions of plasma before recombination
- **Small Scales**: Contributes to temperature variations
- **Velocity**: Plasma moving toward/away from us

### Angular Power Spectrum
- **Multipoles**: Decomposition into spherical harmonics
- **First Peak**: ~200 multipole (1¬∞ scale) - sound horizon
- **Peak Heights**: Reveal matter/energy densities
- **Peak Positions**: Geometric test of universe curvature
