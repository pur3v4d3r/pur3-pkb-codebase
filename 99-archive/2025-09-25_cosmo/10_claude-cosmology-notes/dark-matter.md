# Dark Matter

> *The invisible scaffolding that shapes our universe - comprising ~26.8% of all energy-matter*

## üåå The Invisible Universe

Dark matter is matter that doesn't interact electromagnetically (doesn't emit, absorb, or reflect light) but reveals itself through gravitational effects. Despite being invisible, it outweighs ordinary matter by a factor of **5 to 1**.

## üîç Key Evidence

### 1. Galaxy Rotation Curves ([[Vera Rubin]])
- **Observation**: Stars in galaxy outskirts orbit faster than expected
- **Implication**: More mass present than visible matter accounts for
- **Discovery**: Vera Rubin's groundbreaking work in 1970s

### 2. Gravitational Lensing
- **Weak Lensing**: Statistical distortion of background galaxies
- **Strong Lensing**: Dramatic arc-like distortions
- **Mass maps**: Reveal dark matter distribution independent of visible matter

### 3. Galaxy Cluster Dynamics
- **Bullet Cluster**: Dark matter separated from hot gas in collision
- **Velocity Dispersions**: Galaxy motions require more mass than visible
- **X-ray observations**: Hot gas distribution vs gravitational lensing maps

### 4. Large-Scale Structure ([[Cosmic Web Structure]])
- **Simulations**: Dark matter forms cosmic web first
- **Galaxy Formation**: Ordinary matter falls into dark matter halos
- **Baryon Acoustic Oscillations**: Sound waves in early plasma

### 5. Cosmic Microwave Background ([[Cosmic Microwave Background]])
- **Acoustic Peaks**: Reveal dark matter density parameter
- **Temperature Fluctuations**: Match cold dark matter predictions
- **Polarization**: Additional constraints on dark matter properties

### Leading Candidates

#### WIMPs (Weakly Interacting Massive Particles)
- **Mass**: 10-1000 times proton mass
- **Interaction**: Only gravity and weak nuclear force
- **Status**: Direct detection experiments ongoing
- **Examples**: Neutralinos, axions

- **Properties**: Right-handed neutrinos that don't interact via weak force
- **Mass**: keV scale (warm dark matter)
- **Signatures**: X-ray emission lines

## üî¨ Detection Strategies

### Direct Detection
- **Underground Labs**: Shield from cosmic rays
- **Target Materials**: Xenon, germanium crystals
- **Signature**: Nuclear recoils from WIMP collisions
- **Current Limits**: No confirmed detection yet

### Indirect Detection
- **Gamma Rays**: Dark matter annihilation products
- **Cosmic Rays**: Excess positrons, antiprotons
- **Neutrinos**: From dark matter in Earth/Sun core

### Particle Accelerators
- **LHC**: Search for dark matter production
- **Missing Energy**: Invisible particles escaping detector
- **Constraints**: Ruling out parameter space
