# Nucleosynthesis (Big Bang Nucleosynthesis)

> *The cosmic forge that created the first atomic nuclei - turning pure energy into the building blocks of matter*

Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) is the process that created the lightest atomic nuclei during the universe's first 20 minutes. When the cosmos cooled enough for protons and neutrons to stick together, nuclear fusion briefly turned the entire universe into a stellar core.

- **Goldilocks Zone**: Temperature perfect for nuclear fusion
- **Expansion**: Universe expanding, cooling, and diluting rapidly

## üåü Stellar vs. Primordial Production

### What BBN Made
‚úÖ **Hydrogen-1**: ~75% of all ordinary matter  
‚úÖ **Helium-4**: ~25% of all ordinary matter
‚úÖ **Deuterium**: All deuterium is primordial
‚úÖ **Lithium-7**: Significant fraction is primordial

### What Stars Made Later
- **Heavy Elements**: Carbon, oxygen, iron, etc. (metals)
- **Additional Helium**: Stars convert ~2% more H to He
- **Neutron Capture**: Elements heavier than iron
- **Local Enhancement**: Solar system enriched by stellar evolution

### The Metallicity Connection
- **Population III Stars**: Metal-free, reflect BBN abundances
- **Population II**: Slight metal enrichment, used for BBN tests  
- **Population I**: High metals (like Sun), stellar processing dominates
