# The Horizon Problem

> *Why does the universe look the same in all directions when distant regions were never in contact?*

## ü§î The Puzzle

The Horizon Problem asks a fundamental question: How can opposite sides of the sky have nearly identical temperatures in the [[Cosmic Microwave Background]] when they were never in causal contact? This was one of cosmology's most perplexing mysteries until [[Cosmic Inflation]] provided an elegant solution.

## ‚è±Ô∏è Understanding Cosmic Horizons

### What is a Horizon? ([[Timeline of the Universe]])
- **Particle Horizon**: Maximum distance light could have traveled
- **Causal Connection**: Regions within horizon can communicate
- **Information Limit**: Physics cannot spread faster than light
- **Growing**: Horizon expands as universe ages

### Horizon Size at Recombination
- **Age**: Universe was 380,000 years old
- **Light Travel**: Light had only traveled 380,000 light-years
- **Angular Size**: ~1¬∞ on sky (size of your thumb at arm's length)
- **Implication**: Points >1¬∞ apart were never in contact

### What We Actually Observe
- **Full Sky**: 41,253 square degrees
- **Horizon Patches**: ~10,000 independent regions
- **Each**: Should have random, uncorrelated temperature
- **Reality**: All identical to incredible precision

- **Isolated Systems**: Should have random temperatures
- **No Communication**: Can't equilibrate if never in contact
- **Statistical**: Expect large temperature variations
- **Reality**: Nearly perfect uniformity

## üí° Proposed Solutions (Pre-Inflation)

### Initial Conditions Fine-Tuning
- **Assumption**: Universe started perfectly uniform
- **Problem**: Why? Extremely special initial conditions
- **Probability**: Incredibly unlikely without explanation
- **Philosophy**: Not satisfying scientifically

### Modified Physics
- **Idea**: Maybe light traveled faster in early universe
- **Problems**: Violates relativity, lacks mechanism  
- **Consequences**: Would affect other physics dramatically
- **Status**: No viable models developed

### Chaotic Mixing
- **Concept**: Turbulent mixing before recombination
- **Reality**: No known mechanism for such mixing
- **Timescale**: Too little time for effective mixing
- **Observations**: No evidence for required turbulence
