# The Dark Matter Problem

> *We know it's there, we know how much there is, but we have no idea what it actually is - the universe's greatest hide-and-seek champion*

The Dark Matter Problem isn't just that [[Dark Matter]] is invisible - it's a collection of interconnected puzzles that challenge our understanding of fundamental physics. Despite overwhelming evidence for its existence and precise measurements of its abundance, dark matter remains one of science's most elusive quarries.

## üéØ The Core Problems

### 1. **The Identity Crisis** - What IS Dark Matter?

#### Particle Physics Void
- **No Standard Model Candidate**: No known particle fits the bill
- **Required Properties**: Cold, stable, weakly-interacting, abundant
- **WIMP Searches**: 40+ years of null results from direct detection
- **LHC**: No dark matter particles produced in collisions
- **Theoretical Desert**: Gap between theory and observation

#### The WIMP Paradigm Crisis
```
WIMP (Weakly Interacting Massive Particle) Predictions:
- Mass: 10-1000 times proton mass
- Interaction: Weak nuclear force strength
- Thermal Relic: Left over from Big Bang

Experimental Reality:
- Direct Detection: No confirmed signals
- Indirect Detection: No clear signatures  
- Collider Production: Nothing found
- Parameter Space: Increasingly constrained
```

#### Core-Cusp Problem

- **Observations Show**: Flatter central cores in many galaxies
- **Magnitude**: Factor of 3-10 discrepancy in central regions
- **Universality**: Problem appears in galaxies of all masses

#### Missing Satellites Problem  
- **Theory**: Milky Way should have ~1000 satellite galaxies
- **Reality**: Only ~50 confirmed satellites observed
- **Dark Subhalos**: Simulations produce many more than observed
- **Recent Updates**: More satellites found, but gap remains

#### Too-Big-to-Fail Problem
- **Issue**: Largest satellite dark matter halos should host bright galaxies
- **Observation**: Some massive subhalos appear virtually empty
- **Implication**: Either missing physics or wrong dark matter model
- **Tension**: Challenges cold dark matter predictions

### Beyond WIMPs

#### Axions
- **Origin**: Solution to strong CP problem in particle physics
- **Mass**: Ultra-light (10‚Åª‚Å∂ to 10‚Åª¬≤ eV)
- **Detection**: Convert to photons in magnetic fields
- **Status**: Active searches (ADMX, CAST, IAXO)

#### Sterile Neutrinos
- **Properties**: Right-handed neutrinos, don't interact weakly
- **Mass**: keV scale (warm dark matter)
- **Signatures**: X-ray emission lines, structure formation
- **Evidence**: Some disputed X-ray detections

#### Primordial Black Holes
- **Formation**: Collapsed from density fluctuations in early universe
- **Masses**: Wide range possible (asteroid to stellar)
- **Constraints**: Microlensing, gravitational waves, accretion
- **Status**: Constrained but not ruled out in all mass ranges

#### Self-Interacting Dark Matter (SIDM)
- **Motivation**: Solve small-scale problems
- **Physics**: Dark matter particles interact with each other
- **Effect**: Creates cores, reduces substructure
- **Constraints**: Cross-section must be velocity-dependent

### Fuzzy/Wave Dark Matter

## üéØ Current Detection Strategies

### Next-Generation Direct Detection
- **LZ**: Liquid xenon detector with unprecedented sensitivity
- **DARWIN**: Future multi-ton xenon experiment
- **Directional**: Track dark matter particle directions
- **Multiple Targets**: Silicon, germanium, various nuclei

### Indirect Detection
- **Gamma Rays**: Dark matter annihilation signatures (Fermi, HAWC)
- **Cosmic Rays**: Antimatter signatures (AMS-02, DAMPE)
- **Neutrinos**: Dark matter capture and annihilation (IceCube)
- **Radio**: Axion conversions (SKA, FAST)

### Collider Searches
- **LHC High Luminosity**: Push sensitivity to higher masses
- **Future Colliders**: 100 TeV colliders for broader search
- **Missing Energy**: Invisible particle production signatures
- **Model-Independent**: Search for any new weakly-interacting particles

## ü§î Alternative Explanations

### Modified Gravity Theories ([[Modified Gravity Theories]])

#### MOND (Modified Newtonian Dynamics)
- **Proposal**: Modify gravity law at low accelerations
- **Success**: Explains galaxy rotation curves remarkably well
- **Challenges**: Difficulty with cosmology, cluster dynamics
- **Relativistic Versions**: TeVeS, other covariant theories

#### f(R) Gravity
- **Modification**: Change Einstein-Hilbert action
- **Effect**: Additional scalar degree of freedom
- **Cosmology**: Can mimic dark matter on some scales
- **Constraints**: Solar system tests, gravitational waves

#### Extra Dimensions
- **Braneworld Models**: We live on 4D brane in higher-dimensional space
- **Modified Gravity**: Changes gravity on large scales
- **Dark Matter Mimicry**: Extra-dimensional effects appear as dark matter
- **Constraints**: Precision tests of General Relativity

### Emergent Gravity
- **Concept**: Gravity emerges from more fundamental physics
- **Holographic**: Connection to holographic principle
- **Dark Matter**: Apparent dark matter from emergent gravitational effects
- **Status**: Highly speculative, limited predictive success
