# Dark Energy

> *The mysterious force driving the accelerating expansion of the universe - 68.3% of everything*

## üöÄ The Accelerating Universe

Dark energy is the name given to the unknown cause of the universe's **accelerating expansion**. Discovered in 1998, it represents the most abundant component of the universe yet remains completely mysterious.

## üéØ The Shocking Discovery ([[Discovery of Cosmic Acceleration]])

### Type Ia Supernovae Observations
- **1998 Teams**: Supernova Cosmology Project & High-Z Supernova Search
- **Expectation**: Expansion should be decelerating due to gravity
- **Reality**: Distant supernovae appeared **dimmer** than expected
- **Interpretation**: Universe expansion is **accelerating**

### Nobel Prize 2011
- **Saul Perlmutter, Brian Schmidt, Adam Riess**
- Recognition for "discovery of accelerating expansion through observations of distant supernovae"

## üìä Observational Evidence

### 1. Type Ia Supernovae ([[Cosmic Distance Ladder]])
- **Standard Candles**: Consistent peak luminosity
- **Distance-Redshift**: Deviates from decelerating models
- **Multiple Surveys**: Consistent results across different teams

### 2. Cosmic Microwave Background ([[Cosmic Microwave Background]])
- **Flat Geometry**: Total density equals critical density
- **Matter Density**: Only ~31% (dark + ordinary matter)
- **Missing Component**: ~69% must be something else

### 3. Baryon Acoustic Oscillations
- **Sound Waves**: Frozen in cosmic microwave background
- **Standard Ruler**: Known physical scale in early universe  
- **Distance Measurements**: Confirm accelerating expansion

### 4. Large-Scale Structure Growth
- **Matter Clustering**: Slowed growth rate at late times
- **Weak Lensing**: Gravitational effects trace matter distribution
- **Galaxy Surveys**: Statistical analysis of cosmic structure

- **Controversy**: Disputed whether effect is large enough
- **Complexity**: Requires detailed modeling of cosmic structure

## üåå Cosmic Consequences

### Past, Present, Future

#### Early Universe
- **Negligible**: Dark energy density was tiny
- **Matter Dominated**: Structure could form and grow
- **Transition**: Dark energy became dominant ~5 billion years ago

#### Present Era ([[Lambda-CDM Model]])
- **Dominant Component**: 68.3% of universe
- **Accelerating Expansion**: Distance growth rate increasing
- **Structure Growth**: Slowing due to accelerated expansion

#### Future Evolution
- **Big Rip**: If w < -1, space expands infinitely fast
- **Heat Death**: If w = -1, exponential expansion continues
- **Big Crunch**: If dark energy weakens (unlikely)

### Observational Programs

4. **Connection**: Relationship to dark matter, inflation, or quantum gravity?
