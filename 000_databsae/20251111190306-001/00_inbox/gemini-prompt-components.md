# **PROJECT OUTLINE (DETAILED)**

# PHASE-01 ðŸ”¬ THE "PROMPT TEST BENCH"

To ensure your prompts are high-quality, you need a way to test and iterate on them. We can build a simple, standardized template for this.

- **"Prompt Iteration" Template:** A new note template you'd use to refine a prompt. It would have sections for:
    - **Goal:** What am I trying to achieve?
    - **Prompt v1:** (The text of your first attempt).
    - **Output v1:** (The AI's response).
    - **Analysis v1:** (What was good? What was bad? What was missing?).
    - **Prompt v2:** (Your refined prompt, addressing the issues from v1).
    - **Output v2:** (The new response).
    - **Analysis v2:** (Is it better? Why?).
    - **Finalized Prompt:** (The "production-ready" version).

# PHASE-02 ðŸ§° THE "PROMPT COMPONENT" LIBRARY

Instead of writing common instructions (like personas, tones, or output formats) from scratch every time, we can build a "library" of reusable "LEGO bricks." You could store these as small, individual notes in a dedicated folder (e.g., `Utils/Prompt_Components/`) and pull them into your prompts as needed, or even automate it with Templater.

- **Persona Modules:** A collection of pre-built "Act asâ€¦" directives. We could create detailed persona notes for each pedagogical style you might want (e.g., `Persona-Socratic_Guide.md`, `Persona-Master_Storyteller.md`, `Persona-Systems_Analyst.md`). Each note would define the persona's expertise, tone, and specific behaviors.
- **Tone & Style Modules:** A folder of specific stylistic commands. For example, a note for `Style-Academic.md` (demanding rigor, citations, and formal language) or `Style-Feynman.md` (demanding all concepts be broken down into their simplest possible analogies).
- **Constraint Modules:** A set of notes that define specific "rules" for the AI. For instance: `Constraint-No_Bullet_Points.md`, `Constraint-Web_Research_Required.md`, or `Constraint-Cite_All_Sources.md`.
- **Format Modules:** This would be a collection of output formatting instructions. You already have your callout list, but we could add more, like `Format-Markdown_Table.md`, `Format-Python_Script.md`, or `Format-Dataview_Query.md`.

# PHASE-03 âš™ï¸ THE "OBSIDIAN PROMPTING" WORKFLOW

This is where we build the actual *system* inside Obsidian. Leveraging your stated interest in **QuickAdd** and **Templater**, we can build a "Prompt Launcher" that assembles a complete, complex prompt from all the pieces we've just discussed.

- **The "QuickAdd Prompt Launcher":** We could design a QuickAdd "Macro" that, when triggered, pops up a series of questions:
    1.  `What is your core objective?` (A text field for your main query).
    2.  `Which "Structural Scaffolding"?` (A dropdown selector showing your 8 new SS templates).
    3.  `Which "Persona"?` (A dropdown showing your Persona Modules).
    4.  `Add Context?` (A file-suggester to pick other notes from your vault to add as context).
    5.  `Add Constraints?` (A multi-select for your Constraint Modules).
- **The "Templater Prompt Assembler":** After you fill out the QuickAdd form, a Templater script would run. It would fetch the content from all your selected notes (the SS template, the persona, the constraints) and combine them with your core objective into a *single, perfectly-formatted prompt*, ready to be copied and pasted.
- **The "Prompt Collection" Dataview Dashboard:** You mentioned wanting to track your prompts. We can build a `DataviewJS` dashboard that creates a dynamic table of all your saved prompts. It could show columns for `File`, `Status` (e.g., ðŸ§ªTesting, âœ…Ready), `Purpose`, and what `Model` it's best for (e.g., Gemini, Local/Ollama).

# PHASE-04 ðŸŽ“ THE "PROMPT TECHNIQUE" ENCYCLOPEDIA

Your goal is to learn, and prompt engineering is a field in itself. We can use your *own* **"Deep Exposition" (SS #1)** or **"Case Study" (SS #8)** scaffolds to create a series of masterclass notes that teach you the "why" and "how" behind advanced prompting techniques.

- **Technique Deep Dive: Chain of Thought (CoT):** A detailed report on the "Let's think step-by-step" technique. We would analyze *why* it works (by forcing the model to externalize its reasoning process) and provide examples of *when* to use it (for complex logic, math, or reasoning problems).
- **Technique Deep Dive: Tree of Thoughts (ToT):** A note explaining this more advanced technique, where the AI is prompted to explore *multiple* reasoning branches, self-correct, and then synthesize the best one. This is excellent for creative or strategic problems.
- **Technique Deep Dive: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG):** This is *critical* for your PKB. We can create a detailed guide on how to manually (or even automatically) find relevant notes from your Obsidian vault and "feed" them to the LLM as context, turning it into an expert on *your* personal knowledge.
- **Technique Deep Dive: Few-Shot Prompting:** A guide on the power of providing examples. We've already done this with your "Structural Scaffolding" exemplars. This note would formalize the *why*, explaining how "few-shot" learning (giving 2-3 examples) dramatically improves output quality and consistency.
