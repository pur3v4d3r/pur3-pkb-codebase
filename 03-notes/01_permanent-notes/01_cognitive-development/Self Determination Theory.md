1. Adults need to understand why they need to learn something
2. Adults bring extensive life experience that should inform learning
3. Adults have a psychological need to be responsible for their own learning decisions
4. Adults are ready to learn when training addresses an immediate, relevant problem
5. Adults prefer learning that is problem-centered rather than content-centered
6. Adults are primarily driven by internal rather than external motivators

> [!key-claim]
> SDT maintains that when social and environmental conditions support the satisfaction of these three basic psychological needs, individuals will demonstrate autonomous motivation, high-quality engagement, psychological wellness, and optimal performance. Conversely, when these needs are thwarted, individuals exhibit controlled motivation, disengagement, ill-being, and poor performance.

> [!definition]
> - **Autonomy** refers to the psychological need to experience oneself as the origin of one's own behavior, to act in harmony with one's integrated sense of self rather than being controlled by external forces or internal pressures.
>
> - **Competence** concerns the fundamental human need to feel effective in one's interactions with the environment, to experience mastery and growth in pursuing meaningful challenges.
>
> - **Relatedness** addresses the human need to feel connected to others, to experience oneself as part of a larger community and to have one's existence matter to significant others.

Fulfilling these needs cultivates the highest quality of motivation: [[Intrinsic Motivation]].
