> [!definition]
> - **Key-Term**:: [[Atomic Components]]
> - [**Definition**:: Represent the smallest meaningful unit of prompt functionality.]

 - A [[Role Components|Role]] [[Atomic Components]] might simply state: "*You are a helpful assistant with expertise in Python programming*."
 - A constraint [[Atomic Components|Atomic Component]] might only say: "*Limit your response to 200 words*."

[[Atomic Components]] offer maximum *reusability* and *combinatorial flexibility*â€”you can mix and match them freely to create exactly the prompt you need. 

> However, managing dozens of atomic components for a complex prompt creates cognitive overhead.

> [!analogy]
> **Illuminating Comparison**
> Think of [[Atomic Components|Atomic]] vs. [[Composite Components]] like LEGO bricks vs. pre-assembled LEGO structures.
> 
> *Individual bricks* ([[Atomic Components]]) offer unlimited creative possibilities but require time and skill to assemble into something functional.
> 
> *Pre-assembled structures* ([[Composite Components]]) provide immediate utility but limit customization. 
> 

 > [!helpful-tip]
> Serious LEGO builders maintain **both** a vast collection of ***individual bricks*** for custom creations and a library of ***pre-built subassemblies*** for common structures they use repeatedly.
