> [!key] **to Autonomy in the PKB**
> 1. I shouldn't be afraid of having *misc. Items unfinished or in-progress* throughout the **PKB**. These "Items" **DO NOT** represent bad things or negative concepts to avoid. Instead, *these are avenues for the future self to explore* and get lost in the enticing nature that is knowledge work. When I fret over these unfinished items or tell myself that "*I must do X*" or "*I must do Y*" **I'm being nonautonomous**, **this risks losing the work we've started**.
>    
> 2. In order **to be successfully autonomous** you need to *stop* with making yourself feel bad or guilt ridden over having to many active projects, open work assignment, or tasks to-do, etc. Yes they **DO ADD** to [[Cognitive Load]], however *if managed appropriately through* [[Extended Mind]] theory or [[External-Cognition]].

> [!evidence]
> "True autonomy in a knowledge system is supported by what Nassim Taleb calls the **Anti-Library**. The unread books and open loops in our system should not represent a backlog of guilt (which thwarts autonomy) but a horizon of possibility (which supports autonomy).
