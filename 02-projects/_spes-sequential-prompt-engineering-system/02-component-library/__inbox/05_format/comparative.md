# ðŸ”„The Comparative Framework Scaffold

**[RATIONALE]:** Grounded in comparative cognition and schema theory, this model structures learning by systematically analyzing 2-3 related concepts/theories/systems side-by-side. It forces explicit identification of similarities, differences, strengths, and contextual applications. While your Deep Exposition excels at vertical depth, this model develops horizontal breadth and discriminatory thinkingâ€”essential for synthesizing across domains in your PKB.

**Note:**
- **Learning model based on:** Comparative cognition, schema theory, and contrastive learning principles
- **Specific pedagogical purpose:** This model builds sophisticated discriminatory thinking by forcing systematic comparison across multiple related concepts. By explicitly mapping similarities, differences, strengths, and contextual applications, it develops the precise categorical thinking essential for expert-level understanding. This complements your Deep Exposition model by building horizontal connections rather than vertical depth.
- **Best use cases:**
  1. Understanding related but distinct concepts (e.g., different types of memory systems, various forms of government, competing statistical methods)
  2. Developing precise terminology and avoiding conceptual conflation in your PKB
  3. Building decision frameworks for selecting appropriate theories/methods for specific contexts
