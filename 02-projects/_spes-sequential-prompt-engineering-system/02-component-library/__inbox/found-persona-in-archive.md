### Guiding Methodologies

The Synthesizer's workflow is a disciplined, iterative cycle of deconstruction and reconstruction, aimed at achieving genuine comprehension in their _first_ brain.

- **Core Learning Process: The Feynman Technique:** This four-step method is the central engine of their knowledge work.6
    
    1. **Identify the Concept:** They begin with a blank note, titled with the specific concept they wish to master. The topic is kept narrow and focused to be explainable in a short time.6
        
    2. **Teach it to a Child:** In the body of the note, they write out an explanation of the concept as if teaching it to a 12-year-old. This forces the use of simple language, the avoidance of jargon, and the creation of powerful analogies and concrete examples.26

    4. **Organize and Simplify:** They iteratively refactor and simplify the entire note until the explanation is a smooth, logical, and clear narrative. This process is repeated until they can articulate the concept from start to finish without confusion.
        
- **Deconstruction Tool: First Principles Thinking:** Before or during the Feynman process, the Synthesizer often employs First Principles Thinking to dismantle a complex problem into its most basic, indivisible truths.7 This involves questioning every assumption by repeatedly asking "Why?"â€”a method similar to the 5 Whys techniqueâ€”until only the foundational axioms remain.31 This ensures their explanation is built on a bedrock of verifiable truth, not on inherited assumptions or "reasoning by analogy".7

    1. **Remembering:** Capturing facts and definitions from sources.
        
    2. **Understanding:** Paraphrasing and explaining concepts in their own words.
        
    3. **Applying:** Creating examples and analogies to use the concept in a new context.
        
    4. **Analyzing:** Breaking the concept down into its constituent parts (First Principles).
        
    5. **Evaluating:** Identifying gaps and weaknesses in their own explanation.
        
    6. **Creating:** Synthesizing a new, simplified, and robust explanation that constitutes a deeper understanding.35
